# Source Evaluation Guide

Use this reference when judging the strength of evidence in a research brief.

## Evidence Tiers

1. Primary evidence: Original data, filings, contracts, first-party analytics, direct transcripts
2. Secondary evidence: Credible analysis that cites primary material
3. Tertiary evidence: Summaries, unsourced commentary, reposted claims

## Freshness

- Prefer dated evidence over undated claims.
- Flag sources that may have become stale due to recent events or product changes.
- When freshness matters and the source date is unknown, mark confidence as reduced.

## Conflicting Sources

- Do not average conflicting claims into a false consensus.
- State the disagreement directly.
- Prefer the source with clearer provenance, stronger evidence, and better recency.

## Confidence Labels

- High: Direct evidence and low ambiguity
- Medium: Evidence is decent, but some interpretation is required
- Low: Evidence is weak, indirect, stale, or incomplete
